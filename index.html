Abstract

Large vision-language models struggle with medical video understanding, where spatial precision, temporal reasoning, and clinical semantics are critical. To address this, we first introduce MedVidBench, a large-scale benchmark of 531,850 video-instruction pairs across 8 medical sources spanning video, segment, and frame-level tasks, curated through a rigorous quality assurance pipeline with expert-guided prompting and dual-model validation.

While supervised fine-tuning on MedVidBench improves performance, we show that further applying group relative policy optimization (GRPO) yields consistent additional gains. To handle the heterogeneity of medical video tasks — each requiring distinct reward signals — we design task-specific reward functions with logistic normalization for structured tasks (temporal localization, spatiotemporal grounding) and semantic similarity for open-ended tasks (video summarization, region captioning).

Our two-stage pipeline (SFT + GRPO) trains a single model that achieves strong performance across all tasks, demonstrating the effectiveness of reinforcement learning for heterogeneous medical video understanding.

Method Overview

MedVidBench

531,850 video-instruction pairs across 8 medical sources. Covers video-, segment-, and frame-level tasks with expert-guided QA generation.

Stage 1: SFT

Supervised fine-tuning of Qwen2.5-VL-7B on MedVidBench to build a strong medical video baseline.

Stage 2: GRPO

Group Relative Policy Optimization with task-specific reward functions. Logistic normalization for structured tasks, semantic similarity for open-ended tasks.

Multi-Task Rewards

Heterogeneous reward design: TAL/STG use logistic-normalized IoU; Video Summary/Region Caption use SentenceBERT semantic similarity.
